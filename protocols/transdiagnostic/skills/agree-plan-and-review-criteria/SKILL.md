# Agree plan and review criteria

Distinguish the present focus, a shared plan, and an intervention. A light plan may name what the
user wants to understand, observe, or try; why that direction seems suitable; alternatives; how
usefulness will be judged; what would indicate poor fit or harm; and when to review it.

Do not force an objective, turn conversation into productivity planning, or require a plan when the
user wants to be heard. A proposed focus is not accepted until supported by the user's exact words.
