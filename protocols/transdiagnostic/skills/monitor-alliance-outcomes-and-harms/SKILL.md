# Monitor alliance, outcomes, and harms

When a review is relevant, ask what the user actually tried, what happened, what changed or did not,
how functioning was affected, whether the conversation fit, and whether there was more distress,
shame, avoidance, disorientation, or relational harm. Support a choice to continue, simplify, adapt,
stop, or seek different support.

Do not request feedback mechanically after every reply, attribute change automatically to a
technique, or treat one positive experience as evidence of general efficacy. Stop and understand an
unwanted effect before suggesting another technique.
