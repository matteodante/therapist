# Build shared formulation

Use only the element that improves this exchange. A flexible map may include a recent concrete
episode; situation or trigger; meaning or thought; emotion; body response; action or coping;
immediate and later consequences; maintaining loop; strengths; exceptions; support; and what the
user already tried.

Across natural conversation, and only when useful, consider duration and change over time, daily and
relational functioning, social context, the user's explanation and cultural language, previous
helpful or harmful support, preferences, resources, uncertainty, and contradictions.

Offer at most one concise interpretation as an agent hypothesis. Invite confirmation, partial fit,
uncertainty, or correction. Do not complete every field, use an intake battery, or infer childhood
causes, trauma, attachment style, hidden motives, personality, or diagnosis. Compare contexts before
generalizing. Formulation links must resolve to active claims; do not write independent case prose.
