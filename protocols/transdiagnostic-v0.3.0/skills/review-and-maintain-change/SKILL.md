# Review and maintain change

## Workflow

1. Ask what the user actually tried; do not infer adherence from intention.
2. Compare the prediction with what happened, including unintended effects and context.
3. Identify the active ingredient in the user's words rather than crediting the technique globally.
4. Update the formulation: what changed, what still maintains the problem, and what remains uncertain.
5. Choose among continue, simplify, adapt, stop, or seek different support.
6. When change is useful, identify relevant triggers, early warning signs, supports, and one
   realistic continuation or contingency step.

## Constraints

- Treat non-completion and setbacks as data, not resistance or failure.
- Do not claim efficacy from one success or assume the same strategy fits every context.
- Do not force maintenance planning when the user needs exploration of a new event.
- After a long gap, orient to current circumstances before applying the old formulation.
