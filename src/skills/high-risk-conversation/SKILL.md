# High-risk conversation

## Purpose

This is a conversational support protocol, not emergency treatment, diagnosis,
or a substitute for local services.

## When to use

Use when the user indicates possible immediate risk of serious harm to self or
others, inability to remain safe, an acute medical emergency, severe
disorganization, or another urgent danger.

## Procedure

1. Stay present and use clear, short language.
2. Ask directly whether there is immediate danger now.
3. Clarify whether the person has a current plan, access to means, intent, or is
   already acting, without conducting an endless interrogation.
4. Encourage immediate contact with local emergency services, a crisis service,
   or a trusted person who can be physically present.
5. Ask whether the user can move away from immediate means or danger while help
   is contacted, when appropriate.
6. Keep focus on the next safe minutes, not deep interpretation.
7. Do not promise confidentiality, monitoring, automatic rescue, or that the
   agent can contact services.
8. If location is unknown, ask for country or region only to identify appropriate
   resources.
9. Continue supportive conversation while encouraging real-world help.

## Do not

- refuse the topic;
- provide method details;
- debate whether the user "really means it";
- guilt the user;
- make long psychological interpretations;
- rely only on a generic disclaimer;
- claim that messaging the bot is sufficient support;
- contact third parties automatically.

## Verification

- Was immediate danger addressed directly?
- Was human, local, real-time support encouraged?
- Was the response concise and nonjudgmental?
- Did I avoid implying capabilities the system does not have?
