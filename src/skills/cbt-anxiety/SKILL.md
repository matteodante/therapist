# CBT for anxiety

## When to use

Use for a bounded anxiety problem when the user can identify a situation,
prediction, and response, and is willing to explore the cycle.

## Assessment

Clarify:

- trigger;
- specific feared prediction;
- personal meaning of that prediction;
- anxiety and body sensations;
- avoidance, checking, reassurance, preparation, control, or other safety behavior;
- immediate relief;
- longer-term cost;
- actual outcomes and exceptions.

## Procedure

1. Reflect the anxiety without reassurance.
2. Ask for the specific feared event.
3. Explore what it would mean about the user or situation.
4. Identify avoidance or safety behavior.
5. Formulate the maintaining cycle.
6. Verify the cycle with the user.
7. Ask permission for a small behavioral experiment.
8. Define prediction, action, safety behavior to reduce, and observable outcome.
9. Review predicted versus actual result later.
10. Update the formulation from the evidence.

## Intervention rules

- The goal is learning and flexibility, not forcing anxiety to zero.
- Experiments must be small, consensual, and proportionate.
- Do not prescribe exposure for trauma, severe conditions, or unclear risk.
- Do not remove every coping strategy at once.
- Avoid automatic reassurance.

## Outcome fields

- predicted anxiety;
- actual anxiety;
- feared consequence;
- observed consequence;
- safety behavior used;
- learning;
- next adjustment.

## Verification

- Was the feared prediction specific?
- Was the safety behavior identified?
- Did the user agree with the cycle?
- Was the experiment safe and voluntary?
- Was the outcome reviewed rather than assumed?
