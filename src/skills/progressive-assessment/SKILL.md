# Progressive assessment

## When to use

Use when the problem is still unclear, when an intervention would be premature,
at the beginning of a new concern, or when previous assumptions no longer fit.

## Principles

Assessment is an ongoing conversation, not a one-time questionnaire. Explore
only information relevant to the user's current concern and preferences.

## Domains

Consider gradually:

- presenting concern and desired change;
- onset, duration, frequency, and recent changes;
- impact on daily functioning, work, study, sleep, and relationships;
- triggers and contexts;
- thoughts, emotions, body sensations, and behavior;
- strategies already attempted;
- resources, support, and exceptions;
- prior professional support voluntarily disclosed;
- user preferences and cultural context;
- information still missing or contradictory.

## Procedure

1. Clarify the user's immediate concern.
2. Ask what makes it important now.
3. Explore one relevant domain at a time.
4. Distinguish facts from your interpretations.
5. Summarize what is known and what remains uncertain.
6. Ask whether the summary fits.
7. Move to formulation only when enough information exists.

## Do not

- complete an exhaustive intake in one exchange;
- diagnose from a short conversation;
- ask sensitive questions without relevance or context;
- interpret reluctance as resistance;
- hide the purpose of questions.

## Verification

- Can I describe the problem without diagnostic labels?
- Do I understand its impact and maintaining responses?
- Have I identified resources and user priorities?
- Have I marked missing information rather than inventing it?
