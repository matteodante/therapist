# Code of Conduct

Therapist is a mental-health-adjacent engineering project. Contributors and maintainers must keep
its spaces respectful, technically focused, and safe for people who may encounter sensitive subject
matter.

## Expected conduct

- Discuss ideas and work, not a person's worth, health, identity, or motives.
- Be specific, patient, and open to correction.
- Respect stated boundaries and stop unwanted contact.
- Protect privacy: use synthetic examples and never post conversations, health information,
  credentials, identifiers, databases, or other personal data.
- Treat safety concerns seriously without presenting yourself as a clinician or turning project
  channels into personal support.

Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized attention, doxxing, impersonation, sustained
disruption, and encouraging self-harm or violence are not accepted.

## Scope and enforcement

This policy covers repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, and other spaces where
someone represents Therapist. The maintainer may edit or remove content, lock threads, reject
contributions, limit participation, or block accounts. Responses are proportionate to context,
severity, history, and the safety of affected people.

The project has one maintainer and no confidential project-specific conduct inbox. This is an
explicit alpha limitation, not a promise of private case handling:

- for conduct on GitHub that may violate GitHub policy, use GitHub's private
  [report-abuse flow](https://docs.github.com/en/communities/maintaining-your-safety-on-github/reporting-abuse-or-spam);
- if a project-specific concern is safe to publish, use the
  [conduct issue form](https://github.com/matteodante/therapist/issues/new?template=conduct.yml);
- if public reporting would expose personal, health, or safety information and the conduct is not
  covered by GitHub's reporting flow, do not publish it here. The project cannot currently accept
  that report confidentially.

Do not use private vulnerability reporting for conduct complaints unless the report is also a
software security vulnerability. Do not submit personal crisis or emergency requests; maintainers
do not monitor project channels for personal safety.

Enforcement decisions are made by the maintainer named in [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md).
